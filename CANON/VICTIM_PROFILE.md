# Victim Profile

## Basic Information

**Full Name:** Margot Beaumont
**Nickname/Handle:** @ArtNomadMargot
**Age:** 24
**Nationality:** French
**Occupation:** Art History Graduate Student (on sabbatical)

## Archetype
**Primary:** Cultural Tourist / Aesthete

**Secondary traits:** Intellectual snob, photography obsessive, trust-fund kid pretending to be budget traveler

## The Fatal Flaw
**Core Flaw:** Vanity

**How it manifests:**
- Cannot resist posing with interesting art/architecture
- Prioritizes getting "the shot" over personal safety
- Believes her refined taste elevates her above common tourists

**How it kills them:**
Her insistence on getting the perfect photo beneath the massive vintage tile mosaic, despite visible cracks and staff warnings, leads to her being directly under it when it falls.

## Physical Appearance
- **Height/Build:** 5'5", willowy
- **Hair:** Dark brown, artfully messy bob
- **Distinguishing features:** Oversized vintage glasses, always has a camera around neck, expressive hands when speaking
- **Typical outfit:** Linen culottes, silk scarves, vintage band tees (ironically worn), leather sandals

## Personality

### Positive Traits (that get twisted)
1. Cultured - becomes insufferable snobbery about "authentic" experiences
2. Passionate about art - becomes reckless pursuit of aesthetic moments

### Negative Traits (that seal their fate)
1. Ignores practical concerns for beauty - dismisses cracks in wall as "patina"
2. Condescending - ignores local staff's warnings as uneducated

### Pet Peeves
- "Basic" tourist photos (ironic: dies taking one)
- Mass-produced art

### Fears
- Surface fear: Being seen as a common tourist
- Deeper fear: That her expensive education won't lead to a meaningful life

## Background

### Origin Story
Raised in Lyon by gallery-owner mother and architect father. Never wanted for anything. Got a prestigious scholarship for her PhD but found academia stifling. Took a "gap year" to "find inspiration in the real world" - really just running from dissertation pressure.

### Why They Travel
Seeking authentic artistic experiences to fill her thesis. Also avoiding the reality that she's lost passion for art history and doesn't know what else to do.

### How They Ended Up at This Hostel
Read about its "stunning mid-century Moroccan tile work" in an obscure travel blog. Came specifically to photograph the mosaic in the common room.

## Voice & Speech

### Vocabulary
- Uses words like: "fascinating," "authentic," "derivative," "provincial," "post-modern"
- Avoids words like: "cool," "nice," "whatever," basic adjectives
- Catchphrases: "This is so [city name]" / "You can really see the influence of..."

### Speech Pattern
- Long, complex sentences with subordinate clauses
- French words sprinkled in when English "doesn't capture it"
- Speaks with authority on every topic

### Internal Monologue Style
Constantly framing experiences as future thesis chapters or Instagram captions. Comparing everything to art she's studied.

## Digital Presence

### Social Media
- **Platform of choice:** Instagram (curated) and Twitter/X (art criticism)
- **Follower count:** 12,000
- **Posting style:** Long captions analyzing visual culture, carefully staged "candid" shots
- **Last post before death:** A teaser photo of the mosaic's corner with caption "Found a hidden gem. Full reveal tomorrow."

### Online vs Reality
Presents herself as effortlessly cultured wanderer. Reality: spends hours staging each shot, crippled by comparison to peers doing "real" academic work.

## Relationships

### Family
- Mother texts daily asking about dissertation progress (ignored)
- Father pays her credit card bills without comment

### Friends
- PhD cohort she's grown distant from
- Fellow art travelers met at various museums

### Romantic
- On-off relationship with a sculptor in Paris she's technically "on a break" from

### At the Hostel
- Immediately tries to explain the mosaic's history to uninterested guests
- Clashes with staff who tell her not to touch the art
- Befriends one hostel guest who pretends to care about art to flirt with her

## The Day They Die

### Morning
Wakes early to catch the morning light on the mosaic. Notices the common room is empty - perfect for unobstructed photos.

### Their Last Normal Moment
Adjusting her camera settings, thinking about how the natural light at this hour really brings out the Persian blue in the tiles.

### Final Thoughts
"This is going to be perfect. Wait - what was that sound?"

### Last Words
"Just one more from this angle-" (interrupted by cracking sound)
